Quick reference · How to navigate the map live

1

Open the map and wait for the heatmap to load

Open index.html in Chrome or Firefox. Click through the "How to use" modal. The Flood Index tab loads automatically and begins calculating. Wait for colored squares to fill the O'ahu map (5–10 seconds).

2

Click any colored square to explain a specific neighborhood

Clicking any cell opens a popup showing all 9 factors for that exact location — elevation, soil type, stream distance, iron oxide content, and more. Useful for answering "what about my district?"

3

Switch between scenarios by clicking and hitting Recalculate

The scenario cards are in the Flood Index tab, below the sliders. Click a card, then click Recalculate map. The map updates in a few seconds. Compare Indigenous Land Restoration to Current Conditions or Urban Expansion.

4

The Layers tab has FEMA zones and real stream data

Toggle the FEMA Special Flood Hazard Areas layer to show official designations. Toggle the FVI vs FEMA discrepancy map to show coverage gaps. The waterway network layer shows all 787 real USGS-surveyed stream channels.

5

The Event tab has the March 2026 timeline and sources

If asked for sourcing, the Event tab cites NASA Earth Observatory, NPR, Hawaii Civil Defense, and UH Mesonet for every statistic shown. All data layers cite their official government sources in the layer panel.
